According to recent statistics, Outlook is the third most popular email client, right after Apple Mail and Gmail. Its popularity does not come as a surprise, as it’s a highly versatile platform that can meet the needs of many personal and business users.

Still, Outlook is far from perfect. It has a few significant drawbacks, particularly in the areas of productivity and automation. In this article, you will learn more about those shortcomings and discover some alternatives for Outlook worth looking into.

Why you should explore Outlook alternatives

Outlook is a widely used email service, but it comes with several limitations that can hinder usability and the overall user experience, especially for users seeking advanced features. Some of them include:

Free version drawbacks: Outlook’s free version has distracting ads that can decrease productivity. It also offers limited cloud storage (5 GB), though you get additional 15 GB for email. To get more storage and remove ads, you’ll need to upgrade to a paid plan, which still doesn’t offer essential features.Automation challenges: Outlook offers robust features like Rules and Macros for email automation. However, these features require significant manual setup and ongoing maintenance to keep them effective. For truly hands-off automation, such as meeting scheduling and email composition, you’ll need Microsoft Copilot, which is available as a costly add-on with a steep learning curve. Reliability concerns: Many users report issues with Outlook’s reliability, including crashes and errors. It’s also not well-optimized for all operating systems and can sometimes be slow.

Mailbird

Mailbird has proven itself as a reliable and highly functional email client. The platform’s main selling point is that it’s straightforward and easy to use, so even beginners won’t face a significant learning curve. Still, its productivity tools may not be in-depth enough for power users, especially if you need advanced customization and automation. It mainly offers AI-powered email authoring, which is only available on its premium plans.

Pricing

Front

Front is a communication hub with a modern interface inspired by email clients. It’s an out-of-the-box solution aimed primarily at businesses that need more than an email client. Because of this, you may find its robust features and pricing structure excessive for personal or small business use.

Pricing

Front doesn’t offer a free plan, and you’ll need to splurge quite a bit to use it. You can choose between four tiers:

Pros and cons

Front offers a comprehensive set of features, some of which are overlooked by Outlook and other typical email clients. It can be connected to most major email providers and expand communication beyond them through integrations with messaging platforms like WhatsApp and Telegram.

This communication hub provides detailed insight into your team’s performance and impact, as well as omnichannel messaging that lets you reach team members more easily. Front also offers analytics and reporting features that allow you to track activities and milestones.

By using the platform’s Smart Rules, you can streamline your workflow and automate various tasks. You can also use its AI tools to generate quick responses to customers’ queries. Still, Front lacks in-depth customization options, which can be restrictive if you need to create highly tailored views to improve your workflow.

Spark

With over 14 million users worldwide, Spark is another popular alternative to Outlook. Like Mailbird, it provides a distraction-free zone and a clean interface suitable for beginners. The platform's main caveat is the lack of productivity-enhancing AI-driven features.

Pricing

Spark has a free plan, so individuals and small teams can leverage its features without commitments. If you want to upgrade, there are two plans to choose from:

Premium Individual: $4.99/month billed annuallyPremium Teams: $6.99/month per user billed annually

Premium Individual: $4.99/month billed annually

Premium Teams: $6.99/month per user billed annually

Pros and cons

Spark comes with many useful email organization features. It offers Priority and Pin functions that allow you to highlight important contacts and organize your inbox by sender.

The Smart Inbox feature separates unread mail from read, real messages from ads, and pinned messages from unpinned. It also automatically sorts emails into categories like personal, newsletter, and notification. If you wish to organize messages into folders and use tags and flags, you’ll have to do it manually.

One of the best things about Spark is its free plan. While somewhat limited in features, it lets you test out the environment and use most of the platform’s organization tools. The main drawback of the plan is limited storage, as you only get 5 GB for the whole team. Even if you upgrade to the highest tier, you only get 10 GB per user, which is low for a paid plan.

Another significant limitation of Spark is that its AI features, such as the “+AI” functions for email composition, editing, and summarization, are only available on the paid plan.
