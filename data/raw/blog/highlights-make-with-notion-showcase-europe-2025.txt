Agentic AI has arrived

If last year marked AI's arrival in the workplace, this year ushered in the age of agentic AI—where AI doesn't just assist but takes action. At this year's Make with Notion series, we shared more about Notion 3.0, which marks the shift from Notion as a tool to a teammate. Your Notion Agent can now autonomously handle tedious, multi-step workflows that usually consume hours of your day: writing decision docs from meeting notes, drafting TLDRs, and building, populating, and maintaining databases up to 100× faster than doing it manually.

We also previewed Custom Agents. Your Notion Agent can do a lot and takes action when you ask it to. But Custom Agents can be shared across teams, scoped to specific databases or teamspaces, and triggered automatically by schedules or events. Imagine waking up to your daily brief prepared, last night's bug reports already triaged, and new employee onboarding kicked off—all before you've had your morning coffee. And with MCP (Model Context Protocol) integration, you can access content from Notion and other connected apps, like Slack and Jira, while working within other tools. Coding in Cursor or designing in Figma? Your Notion context comes with you.

Custom Agents are currently in early access with select customers and will be available more broadly in early 2026. We can’t wait to see what you all build. If you want to learn more, sign up here.

Context is (still) king: Why knowledge unlocks AI's potential

Some of the most impactful sessions were our customer panels on knowledge management in the age of AI. In partnership with independent market research agency Vanson Bourne, we surveyed hundreds of business leaders across Europe earlier this year to understand how companies are managing organizational knowledge and integrating AI.

It turns out businesses of all sizes still struggle with knowledge scattered across disconnected tools—and it's costing real money. In the UK, France, and Germany alone, organizations lose up to €16,000 per employee every year due to time wasted searching for information. That's not overhead or opportunity cost. That's money walking out the door.

So what’s the the critical insight? Nscale CPO Dan Bathurst put it simply: “You can use AI as much as you want, but if you don't have relevant context, you’re essentially going to get slop out the other side.” All those powerful AI agents we just talked about are only as good as the knowledge they can draw from. We can't expect agentic AI to autonomously handle complex tasks if information isn’t documented and centralized.

Snir Yarom, CTO of Taxfix, emphasized that investing in taxonomy structure and AI-friendly content early pays massive dividends later. "Having a centralized knowledge base may be a big upfront investment," he said, "but it allows you to execute faster down the road."

Knowledge management isn't just about finding information faster. It's about building the foundation that lets AI—and your team—do their best work.
