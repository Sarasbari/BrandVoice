Removing your Gmail account from an iPhone

If you connect your Gmail account to your iPhone, you’ll get emails to the device’s native Mail app, where you can manage them alongside other accounts. You can add or remove accounts from the Settings app, where you’ll find an option to delete your Gmail account. Take the following steps to do so:

Open Settings and scroll down to MailGo to AccountsHere, you’ll see all accounts connected to the Mail app, so tap Gmail to access additional settingsTap Delete Account at the bottom of the menu and confirm the deletion when prompted

Open Settings and scroll down to Mail

Go to Accounts

Here, you’ll see all accounts connected to the Mail app, so tap Gmail to access additional settings

Tap Delete Account at the bottom of the menu and confirm the deletion when prompted

From the same menu, you can choose which Gmail data you want to sync with your phone. If you only want to temporarily stop receiving emails but might need the account in the future, you can toggle Mail off.

How to remove Gmail accounts from Android

Deleting your Gmail account from an Android phone is quite similar to doing it on an iPhone and involves three quick steps:

Open Settings > AccountsTap the Google/Gmail account you want to removeTap Remove Account and confirm your choice

Open Settings > Accounts

Tap the Google/Gmail account you want to remove

Tap Remove Account and confirm your choice

Note that Android is Google’s operating system, so naturally, the company wants you to use their accounts on the phone. That’s why removing your Google account deletes not only Gmail but also other connected services and data. It would be the same as removing an iCloud account from an iPhone.

Google confirmed that the only way to stay signed out of Gmail on Android is to remove the entire Google account, so it might be a good idea to back up your data beforehand.

How to delete an account from the Gmail app

Besides removing your Gmail account from the device’s settings, you can do it from the provider’s app. The following steps show how to delete an account from the iOS app:

Open the Gmail app and tap your profile icon in the upper-right cornerTap Manage accounts on this device (not to be confused with the Manage your Google Account option above it, which will be explained later)Select Remove from this device and confirm the removal

Open the Gmail app and tap your profile icon in the upper-right corner

Tap Manage accounts on this device (not to be confused with the Manage your Google Account option above it, which will be explained later)

Select Remove from this device and confirm the removal

The steps are slightly different on the Android version of Gmail:

Tap the hamburger icon and go to SettingsTap the three-dot icon in the upper-right corner and go to Manage AccountsChoose the account you want to remove and tap Remove account

Tap the hamburger icon and go to Settings

Tap the three-dot icon in the upper-right corner and go to Manage Accounts

Choose the account you want to remove and tap Remove account

Removing a Gmail account from your phone doesn’t delete the account itself, regardless of your chosen approach. If you want to delete your Gmail account completely, you must take some extra steps.

How to delete a Gmail account permanently from mobile apps

iOS and Android versions of Gmail’s app let you delete your account in a similar way, so we’ll use the former to outline the steps:

Open the Gmail app and tap your profile iconTap Manage your Google AccountGo to Data & privacy and scroll down to Download or delete your dataTap Delete a Google serviceEnter your Google account password to verify your identityTap the bin icon next to GmailChoose an email address to which you want to receive a verification email to confirm the deletionSign into your chosen account and verify it

Open the Gmail app and tap your profile icon

Tap Manage your Google Account

Go to Data & privacy and scroll down to Download or delete your data

Tap Delete a Google service

Enter your Google account password to verify your identity

Tap the bin icon next to Gmail

Choose an email address to which you want to receive a verification email to confirm the deletion

Sign into your chosen account and verify it

When you complete the verification process, your Gmail account will be deleted. Other Google services will still be active, so you can delete them separately by following the above steps. If you don’t want to use any of Google’s services anymore, you can delete your entire Google account.

Note that if you’re using Android, you will no longer be able to download anything from the Play Store until you create another Google account and sign in.
