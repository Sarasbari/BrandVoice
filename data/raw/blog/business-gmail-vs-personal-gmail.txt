Personal vs. business Gmail: Feature availability

The first notable feature you get with every paid Gmail plan is a custom email address. Instead of your email ending with @gmail.com, it can include your company name. This might contribute to your business’s reputation and trustworthiness.

All business tiers also remove ads from your inbox, which are quite obstructive and invasive in the free account. They’re also based on your activity, which Google tracks to target you with personalized ads, so this raises some concerning privacy questions.

If you subscribe to a business account, you’ll get access to the Admin Console—an account management platform unavailable with the free plan. Among various options, it lets you set specific account permissions for other users in your organization to limit information sharing. For instance, you can restrict file sharing to ensure sensitive data can’t be sent outside certain departments.

There aren’t many feature differences besides the above when it comes to Gmail specifically. Note that other Google Workspace tools will get expanded usability if you upgrade, increasing the plans’ value for money.

Business vs. personal Gmail: Storage

A free Google account comes with 15 GB of storage shared across all platforms, including Gmail. This is likely not enough for an average business user because Google Drive files might fill up the space quite quickly.

Each business tier comes with expanded storage:

Once you commit to a plan, you can’t change the storage separately. Only the Enterprise plan allows you to request additional space as needed.

Overall, storage isn’t Gmail’s strong point, especially since many competitors are more generous with it. For instance, Outlook’s lowest-tier business account gets you 50 GB of dedicated email storage alongside 1 TB of drive storage for the same price as Google’s Business Starter.

Gmail for business vs. personal account: Customer support

With a free Gmail account, you get 24/7 support for emergency technical assistance. Support is available through several channels, most notably by phone and chat. The average response time is around four hours with standard support, so you’ll need to upgrade to shorten it.

The problem is priority support isn’t included in any of the fixed business plans. You must buy it separately, in which case you can choose from two options:
