Can Outlook automatically sort emails into folders?

Yes, Outlook can automate sorting emails into folders with its built-in feature called rules. This feature is designed to simplify and streamline managing your Outlook inbox. It can be super helpful if you have a high volume of emails you need to organize daily or if you simply want to keep your primary inbox clutter-free.

What are Outlook rules, and how do they work?

Outlook rules are automated instructions for managing incoming or outgoing emails. All you need to do is set specific conditions, and when an email matches that criteria, Outlook will detect it and automatically perform the action you’ve defined.

With Outlook rules, you can automate a decent chunk of tedious tasks, including:

Highlighting emails with a specific keywordDeleting or archiving emailsFlagging emails from important sendersAutomatically assigning categories for a specific keywordSetting up custom alertsForwarding emails to target recipientsSending emails to folders

How to automatically move emails to a folder in Outlook

As mentioned earlier, one of the most useful ways to leverage Outlook rules is by automatically sending your incoming emails to specific folders. For example, you could create a rule that routes messages from your boss and coworkers directly to your Work folder. Setting up a rule takes just a minute, and it saves you the time you’d otherwise spend manually sorting your inbox.

Note: Outlook doesn’t let you create rules from your iOS or Android app. To set them up, you’ll need to use your computer or open Outlook on the web.

2. Organize emails with categories

Another way to organize your Outlook inbox is to use categories. They can complement the folders well by adding color-coded labels to your emails that help you filter through messages later.

The difference between categories and folders is that they don’t move emails out of your inbox like folders do. You can also add multiple categories to a single email if it relates to more areas—and here’s how:

3. Use Outlook’s Focused Inbox

Outlook’s Focused Inbox can separate the most important emails from the less relevant ones. This can be a helpful feature if you don’t want to overlook key emails because of a cluttered inbox or too many promotional emails.

If you’d like to activate the Focused Inbox, do this:

Select the settings icon at the top rightChoose LayoutClick Sort messages into Focused and OtherSelect Save

Select the settings icon at the top right

Choose Layout

Click Sort messages into Focused and Other

Select Save

After that, Outlook will automatically sort emails into Focused and Other depending on how it assesses their priority. The only issue is that it might route an important email to the Other folder. If that happens, move it to the Focused Inbox manually.

4. Flag important emails

If you receive a large influx of emails, Outlook flags can help you keep track of those that need your attention. Whether you want to revise the message again or send a reply, Outlook will highlight it and add it to the Microsoft To Do app.

To flag an email, just click the flag icon next to it. You can also set up a follow-up reminder by right-clicking the flag and selecting a follow-up time.
