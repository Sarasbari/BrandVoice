Search engines have revolutionized how people seek products and services — and they’re crucial to helping consumers discover your business.

No matter how much attention your team puts into initiatives like research, product development, and sales, you can’t achieve maximum market integration if your site doesn't appear near the top of Google's leaderboards. Few people scroll to page two on a search engine results page (SERP), so you need to prioritize your rankings for online visibility.

To make it to the top of the SERPs, marketers turn to a tactic known as search engine optimization (SEO). A rock-solid SEO campaign enables you to compete with other brands on search engines, monitor your web traffic, and tweak online content strategies for the highest rate of success.

What’s an SEO campaign, and what are SEO strategies?

An SEO campaign encompasses all the tasks and techniques teams use to increase their web page's ranking on search engines like Google and Bing. The goal of any SEO campaign is to drive more organic traffic to your website by getting close to — or nabbing — the number one spot on multiple SERPs.

While there are dozens of brand-specific components in an SEO campaign, the core strategies include researching search traffic, identifying relevant keywords, and analyzing competitors. These steps help teams develop consistent, high-quality, and high-converting content.

What’s SEO campaign management?

The difference between an SEO campaign and SEO campaign management is that the former defines your strategy for success, while the latter serves as a management process as teams action the campaign. Through SEO campaign management, you can review metrics related to new content optimization plans — such as average organic traffic, click-through rates (CTRs), and backlinking on authoritative sites — to see if a campaign has a positive impact. If SEO strategies don't appear to influence SERP performance, it's time to collect more data and develop new tactics for successful rankings. And SEO campaign management also includes monitoring algorithm adjustments on search engines and assessing how these changes affect the efficacy of an SEO plan.

What makes an effective SEO content strategy?

SEO analysts pore over quantitative metrics to get an objective read on your website's performance and measure the impact of various content optimization techniques. By closely monitoring these categories, your team gleans actionable insights into SEO successes and sore spots, contributing to a more effective, data-driven SERP strategy.

Here are a few more components of an effective optimization-based content strategy:

Organic traffic — any time a visitor lands on your website through a search engine, it’s considered organic traffic. SEO strategies are laser-focused on driving up a website's organic traffic by working with search engine algorithms to boost rankings. High organic traffic helps companies cut their advertisement spending and connect with customers who are actively searching for relevant products, services, and information. Keywords — keywords are the specific words or phrases people type into a search engine. SEO strategies involve identifying highly relevant keywords for your company's target audience — as well as your competitors’ — and using this information to craft content with strategically placed phrases. Bounce rate — a site's bounce rate measures how many visitors end up on your site and quickly leave (or "bounce"). If a webpage has abnormally high bounce rates, it suggests there are problems such as poor content quality, slow loading speeds, or a clunky page layout. CTR — unlike bounce rates, CTRs focus on the number of search engine users who click your website versus competing options for a specific keyword. High CTRs plus a long average time spent on a site (or the user retention rate) are the gold standards for successful SEO content strategies.Backlinks — sometimes called inbound links, backlinks are hyperlink tabs to your webpage from other sites. Backlinks matter because they alert search engine algorithms that you're providing high-quality information, which often strengthens your credibility and site visibility on SERPs.

SEO planning tips: Developing an SEO content strategy

Because SEO campaigns focus on predicting the preferences of advanced algorithms, creating an effective strategy might seem complicated. But you don't need expert coding chops to craft an actionable plan. All you need when creating an SEO content campaign are actionable data points to develop your strategy and measure results.

Here’s a four-step guide to crafting an effective strategy.

1. Establish your target audience

Before brainstorming content ideas, get a clear picture of your target demographic. Use any relevant data you have on file — including consumer personas, user stories, and current search trends — to identify the people most likely to search for optimal keywords.

Also imagine the type of online content your readers expect, including the ideal tone of voice and average length per post. And consider any common interests or pain points across your audience to tailor your content for superior engagement.

2. Perform keyword research and competitive analysis

After establishing your target audience, run an analysis on relevant and high-traffic keywords in your niche and identify the dominant competing pages. Use this information to understand what other sites get right, and analyze potential keyword trends or knowledge gaps to seize. For optimal organization, consider using templates designed for competitive analysis or keyword tracking to keep tabs on competitor strategies and develop content generation ideas.

3. Develop and implement content optimization strategies

After you gather insights into high-ranking keywords and competitor strategies, implement an effective content strategy across your company's online presence. Optimize blogs, social media posts, and landing pages for target keywords, and set a consistent publishing schedule to ensure there's a constant stream of fresh content. Then research strategies to increase backlinks, such as leveraging guest blog opportunities, fixing broken links, and spotlighting customer testimonials.

4. Monitor results and adjust accordingly

Once your content optimization plan is in full swing, pay attention to metrics like your webpage's CTR, bounce rate, and organic traffic, and assess how different SEO tactics work. Note significant algorithm changes at major search engines, and determine whether these updates affect your site's competitiveness. If site traffic is flat or declining, return to the drawing board and determine your next strategic move.
