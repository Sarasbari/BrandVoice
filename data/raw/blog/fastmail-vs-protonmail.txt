Proton Mail vs. Fastmail: User interface

Both providers have simple and clean interfaces that contribute to a satisfactory user experience.

Fastmail makes managing your inbox easier with features like threaded conversations and unlimited folder additions. The color customization tools help add a personal touch. Note that some users have complained about issues like delayed delivery and no image-zoom-in feature and felt that the user interface needed to be more optimized. Some also expressed frustration over being unable to open PDF files on the platform.

Proton Mail flaunts a modern dashboard with text grouping and theme customization options. You can also switch between column and row layouts depending on what works for you. While the provider has significantly modernized the user interface and provides an overall smooth email experience, a handful of users say they experienced lags (loading or notification) with Proton Mail.

Proton Mail vs. Fastmail: Ease of setup and migration

Proton Mail’s account setup is generally straightforward but requires completing a 10-step process to access your new inbox. In addition to your name and password, you’ll be asked to provide more information, such as a display name, contact number, and a different email address for recovery. Setting up a free Proton Mail account is relatively quick, but upgrading to a paid tier requires additional steps to customize your new paid features.

Proton Mail also offers various migration tools, including Easy Switch, designed to help you migrate your emails from other providers. However, the migration guides are somewhat technical, and some users might find the process difficult to follow.

In comparison, Fastmail’s setup is smoother, quicker, and more intuitive. The simple signup page requires only minimal information to get you started. It excels in migration, with a robust tool for importing emails, contacts, and calendars from other services in minutes.

Overall, Fastmail prioritizes a quicker onboarding and migration experience. Proton Mail’s security-centric approach, though complex, may be a trade-off users find worthwhile for enhanced privacy.

Proton Mail vs. Fastmail: Workflow optimization

Proton Mail offers basic folder and label organization, which works well for simple email activities because it requires manual input. You can also use its filter feature to auto-manage incoming mail. However, its customization options for inbox views are limited, making it less suitable for handling high volumes of emails.

Fastmail, on the other hand, offers more extensive folder and label management, threaded conversations, and color customization for folders to enhance the work experience. You can also customize your inbox view and create folders for emails you search for frequently. That said, setting up your inbox for a seamless workflow experience can take a while.

Proton Mail offers a cohesive experience when integrating with its ecosystem of tools, but third-party integrations can be a challenge. Fastmail supports IMAP/SMTP protocols that make it easier to integrate with a wide range of external apps.

Proton Mail primarily focuses on security and offers a basic interface that makes it easy to get things done. However, this means that its automation features are limited. It offers tools like email auto-forwarding and auto-filtering, but you must set up custom rules manually to activate them.

In comparison, Fastmail’s automation is limited to trainable spam filters that classify spam and custom rules for managing your inbox. The platform also offers a snooze option for emails, which may interest business professionals and productivity-focused users, though the feature is unavailable to Basic plan subscribers.
